---

## 1. THE PROBLEM — What Pain Are We Solving?

Every Nepali family organises ceremonies. Finding reliable, fairly priced service providers is still:

- ❌ Based entirely on **word of mouth** — no transparency
- ❌ **No price comparison** — customers pay whatever vendors quote
- ❌ **No reviews or ratings** — no accountability for bad service
- ❌ **No booking confirmation** — verbal agreements fall through
- ❌ **No centralised platform** — customers call 10 different people for 10 services
- ❌ Service providers have **no digital presence** — they lose business because they can't be found

**The result?** Families overpay, get cheated, or scramble at the last minute. Service providers lose customers because they are invisible online.

---

## 2. THE SOLUTION — What Is Utsav?

**Utsav** is a mobile-first, ecommerce-style service marketplace that connects families planning ceremonies with verified local service providers — photographers, caterers, decorators, tent suppliers, venues, bands, and more.

Think of it as **Daraz + eSewa — but for ceremony services**.

- Customers browse, compare, and book services in one place
- Providers get a digital shop, manage bookings, and grow their business
- Payments happen securely through eSewa, Khalti, and bank transfer
- Reviews and ratings create trust and accountability

## 4. BUSINESS MODEL — How Does Utsav Make Money?

### 💰 Revenue Stream 1 — Transaction Commission (Primary)
- Utsav charges **8–15% commission** on every confirmed booking
- If a customer books a NPR 50,000 photography package → Utsav earns NPR 4,000–7,500
- This is the Daraz model applied to services

### 💰 Revenue Stream 2 — Provider Subscription Plans
- FREE plan: List up to 3 services, basic profile
- **SILVER — NPR 999/month**: Up to 10 listings, priority placement, verified badge
- **GOLD — NPR 2,499/month**: Unlimited listings, home page feature, analytics dashboard, promotional boosts
- Even 500 Gold subscribers = **NPR 12.5 Lakhs/month** in recurring revenue

### 💰 Revenue Stream 3 — Featured & Promoted Listings
- Providers pay to appear at the top of search results and home page
- Event-based boosts during peak wedding/festival seasons (Dashain, Tihar, Falgun)
- Starting at **NPR 500–5,000 per boost campaign**

### 💰 Revenue Stream 4 — Verified Badge & Trust Score
- Providers pay NPR 1,500–3,000 for background verification and a verified checkmark
- Builds trust, providers get more bookings, they renew — recurring revenue

### 💰 Revenue Stream 5 — Cancellation & Insurance Fees
- Small processing fee on cancellations (2–3% of booking value)
- Future: Event booking insurance product in partnership with a Nepali insurer

### 💰 Revenue Stream 6 — B2B Corporate Events
- Companies booking venues, caterers, and decorators for corporate events, product launches, team events
- Higher ticket sizes, less price sensitivity

### 💰 Revenue Stream 7 — Data & Insights (Future)
- Aggregated, anonymised data on event trends, pricing, and demand sold to hotels, event venues, and suppliers
- Only after reaching scale

## 5. SWOT ANALYSIS

### ✅ STRENGTHS
- **First-mover advantage** — no organised player in this exact space in Nepal
- **Deep cultural fit** — built specifically for Nepali ceremonies, not adapted from a foreign product
- **Two-sided network effect** — more providers attract more customers, which attracts more providers
- **Low provider acquisition cost** — thousands of photographers, caterers, decorators are already on Facebook; easy to onboard
- **Asset-light model** — Utsav does not own any services, it just connects; low overhead
- **Recurring revenue** — subscriptions + commissions create predictable income
- **eSewa/Khalti integration** — payment infrastructure already exists and is widely trusted

### ⚠️ WEAKNESSES
- **Cold start problem** — marketplace needs both sides to launch; the chicken-and-egg challenge
- **Trust gap** — Nepali customers are used to verbal agreements; digital booking requires behaviour change
- **Low digital literacy in rural areas** — service works best in urban areas first
- **Provider reluctance** — older vendors may resist paying commission or going online
- **Cash-heavy culture** — many transactions still happen in cash; digital payment adoption is still growing
- **Small team risk** — early stage dependency on key people

### 🚀 OPPORTUNITIES
- **28.6% annual growth** in Nepal's digital commerce — the wave is coming regardless
- **Government's National Startup Policy 2024** — NPR 1 Billion startup fund, 3% subsidised loans available
- **No direct competitor** — the gap is wide open
- **Diaspora demand** — NRNs planning Nepal ceremonies from abroad desperately need a trusted online platform
- **Destination weddings** — foreign couples spending USD 15,000–50,000 on Nepal weddings need an organised booking platform
- **Expand to India's Nepali market** — 4+ million Nepalis in India planning ceremonies back home
- **WhatsApp/social media integration** — providers already communicate on WhatsApp; integration = easy adoption
- **Government digital push** — Digital Nepal Framework actively promoting app-based services

### 🔴 THREATS
- **Copycat risk** — if successful, larger players (Indian, regional) may enter
- **Provider disintermediation** — once customer and provider connect, they may bypass the platform for repeat bookings
- **Regulatory changes** — payment regulations, commission caps, data laws
- **Economic downturns** — ceremonies are reduced during economic hardship (as seen post-COVID)
- **Seasonal dependency** — 70% of weddings happen in 4 months (Magha, Falgun, Baisakh, Kartik)
- **Internet reliability** — outages in rural Nepal may affect user experience
